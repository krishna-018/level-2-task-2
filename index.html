About C V Raman

Sir Chandrasekhara Venkata Raman, also known as C V Raman, was an Indian physicist who made groundbreaking discoveries in the field of light scattering. He was born in Tiruchirapalli, India on November 7, 1888, and passed away on November 21, 1970. Raman received numerous awards and honours for his contributions to science, including the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1930.

Scientific Achievements

  • Discovery of the Raman effect (1928)
  • Discovery of the Compton effect (1928)
  • Discovery of the Raman-Nath theory of diffraction (1930)
  • Discovery of the quantum photon spin (1933)

Honours and Awards

  • Nobel Prize in Physics (1930)
  • Bharat Ratna (1954)
  • Knighthood (1929)
  • Franklin Medal (1941)
  • Padma Bhushan (1954)
